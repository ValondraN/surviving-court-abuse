Resource Library

Practical tools you can use without exposing personal case details. Educational only, not legal advice.

Documentation

Write notes that stay factual, consistent, and easy to reference.

Clarity
  • Fact-only event logDate, time, location, who was present, what was said or done.
  • Impact statement (short)What changed, what was disrupted, what you had to do next.
  • Neutral language bankSwap emotional words for observable descriptions.
Tip: If you are activated, write only facts first. Return later for impact.

Communication boundaries

Scripts that reduce escalation and keep you traceable.

Boundaries
  • BIFF-style repliesBrief, informative, friendly, firm.
  • Do not engage baitOne sentence, one point, no defense speech.
  • Delay scripts“I will respond after I review the schedule.”
Goal: fewer openings for argument, more clarity.

Court-related trauma education

Why your body stays on alert and how to reduce the cost of constant vigilance.

Healing
  • Trauma responses in litigationHypervigilance, rumination, shutdown, and why they make sense.
  • Regulation before decisionsHow to calm your system before writing, responding, or filing.
  • Energy protectionHow to stop the conflict from living in your body all day.
Reminder: you are not weak, you are responding to prolonged stress.

Supporting children

Keep kids out of adult conflict while staying emotionally present.

Children
  • Language for loyalty bindsValidate feelings without validating narratives.
  • When a child reports messagesHow to respond without interrogating or escalating.
  • Safety and stability routinesPredictable structure to reduce anxiety.
Children need calm structure, not adult strategy talks.
